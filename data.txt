Event: 1969 Moon Landing. Fact: On July 20, 1969, the Apollo 11 mission successfully landed humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above. It marked a giant leap for mankind.

Event: 1776 American Independence. Fact: The Declaration of Independence was adopted by the Continental Congress on July 4, 1776. This document announced that the thirteen American colonies were no longer part of the British Empire.

Event: 1945 End of World War II. Fact: World War II officially ended in September 1945 after the surrender of Japan. The conflict was the deadliest in human history and led to the creation of the United Nations to prevent future wars.

Event: 1989 Fall of the Berlin Wall. Fact: The Berlin Wall, which divided East and West Berlin for 28 years, was opened on November 9, 1989. This event symbolized the end of the Cold War and the beginning of German reunification.

Event: 1912 Titanic Sinking. Fact: The RMS Titanic struck an iceberg in the North Atlantic and sank on April 15, 1912. More than 1,500 people lost their lives in one of the most famous maritime disasters in history.

Event: 1955 Civil Rights Movement. Fact: Rosa Parks refused to give up her seat on a bus in Montgomery, Alabama, on December 1, 1955. Her brave action sparked the Montgomery Bus Boycott and a major shift in the American Civil Rights Movement.

Event: 1928 Discovery of Penicillin. Fact: Alexander Fleming discovered the first true antibiotic, Penicillin, in 1928. This discovery revolutionized medicine and saved millions of lives by successfully treating bacterial infections.
